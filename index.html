LEAP through the leap year!

Honoring the start of the decade being a leap year, we have divided innovative project themes into four categories, each starting with one of the letters in "leap":

Lifestyle

Is there a part of your (or someone else's) life you think you can improve through technology? Make that idea a reality here!

Environment

Global warming and climate change have all of us concerned, so solutions to this problem are absolutely welcomed at duckHacks!

Automation

Can a robot maid clean my room for me? Maybe not, but automate your arduous tasks for serious points here.

Productivity

Nothing is as exhilarating as completing four final projects in one night. Help us thwart our own procrastination.

Why come to duckHacks 2020?

duckHacks is back, and better than ever! Come through to the fifth rendition of this event!

Build your ideas

Have that idea that you never got around to making a project out of? Do you think you have a solution to a problem, but don't know where to start? duckHacks is the perfect environment for executing that project, where talented hackers partner up with bright-eyed thinkers to implement new and exciting solutions.

Get recognized

This year, duckHacks has partnered with the Stevens Career Center to bring some of the top companies recruiting from Stevens to duckHacks! Make sure to bring your resume and your knowledge as you impress some of Stevens' top supporters and grow your own professonal network.

Win prizes

With four categories, and winner and runner-up prizes in each category, there are many opportunities to win something nice to take home with you after the event. Prizes will be themed for each category, and will be announced in the near future.

Eat up

We'll be providing free food for the duration of the event, with all the snacks you could ask for as well! Look out for midnight treats and plenty of caffeine to keep you going on your hack too.

All of these and more are on offer at our annual duckHacks 2020 event. Get your project to the finish line to be judged on criteria such as originality, practicality, implementation, and presentation!

Frequently Asked Questions

  • When and Where?

    DuckHacks is in the Edwin A Stevens Building (EAS) from 11 AM on March 28th, 2020 till 4PM on March 29th, 2020. If you need help locating EAS, check out the Stevens Campus Map: Stevens Campus Map (PDF).

  • How much does it cost?

    $0. It's free!

  • What if I don't know how to code?

    That's ok! We have interactive workshops to get you started, and remember you will have teammates that will be there to support you through the process.

  • Is there a theme?

    Since it's a leap year, our theme is LEAP, which stands for Lifestyle, Environment, Automation and Productivity. At this year's duckHacks, hackers are encouraged to solve some of the existing problems surrounding these categories.

  • Do I need a team?

    You can go solo, or be part of a team (maximum size of 4). You can come with your team, or we can assign you to one when you arrive at your request! This will be a great opportunity to meet new people and make new friends.

  • Who can come?

    All currently enrolled college students can attend this year's DuckHacks!

  • What is a Hackathon?

    A hackathon is an event where people come together to solve a problem through programming and design. What's the best part of it all? You get to choose what problem you want to solve -- the options are endless!

  • Any other questions?

    Have more questions? E-mail us at: stevensduckhacks@gmail.com
